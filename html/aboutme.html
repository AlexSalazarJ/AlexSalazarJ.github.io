about me pt1

I graduated high school in 2011 and then enlisted in the Marine Corps for six years. While enlisted, I was given the opportunity travel around the world. I've lived in Europe, Africa, and the Middle East. While living abroad, I made sure to do plenty of traveling, I even climbed Mount Kilimanjaro, which I think I have already mentioned. I got out in 2017 and then started my first term at PSU that fall. I tried out CS161 with David Ely and fell in love with the class so I ended up majoring in computer science. I am in the MECOP internship program and am currently working at my second internship. My first internship was at ON Semiconductor, which I was not a fan of. Coworkers were great, the company was awesome, but I did not like the job. I currently work at Garmin for my second internship and I love it. I work down in Newport, OR, working on their marine technology. I love it and am so happy to have been given the opportunity to work here. The only problem is the commute. All my work at Garmin has been in C and Python, while at ON Semiconductor I worked with a lot of SQL. In general, I am most comfortable with C (I'm rusty with C++ but sure I could brush up on it) and Python. Something that I am not sure on how to approach is actually web development. It just seems like there is so much to learn. I understand that as computer scientists and software engineers we are always learning something new, but it just feels like web development has a huge barrier with all of the different tools being used.
